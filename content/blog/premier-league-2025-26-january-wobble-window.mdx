# Premier League 2025-26 in January: Arsenal's Goals Dried Up, and the Title Race Woke Back Up

Since October I have been saying the same thing about Arsenal in slightly different words, which is that a team built on not conceding has one real way to lose a lead, and it is a spell where the attack goes quiet and the 1-0 wins turn into 0-0 draws. I kept flagging it, December did not trigger it, and then January arrived and did exactly the thing I had been warning about. The vulnerability was not a surprise when it came, because it was the vulnerability I had been describing for three months. It was still uncomfortable to watch it actually happen.

The shape of the month was a run of games where the defense kept doing its job and the attack simply stopped scoring. Arsenal were held to a couple of goalless draws, dropped a home game they would have been favored to win, and then drew a couple more where the story was the same, a solid enough performance with nothing at the end of it. None of it was a collapse. The team did not fall apart, the clean sheets mostly kept coming, and that is precisely the trap of a defensive title team, because the floor is so high that the bad spell looks respectable on paper while the points quietly leak away. A lead that at its healthiest might have stretched toward nine points was suddenly down to two, and a race that had felt close to settled was wide open again.

## Manchester City did the thing City always do

The team that made Arsenal pay was Manchester City, and I want to be honest that I have been saying since the autumn that I would not exhale with City within touching distance, precisely because this is the pattern. City spend the first half of the season staying in range without dominating the conversation, and then they wait for the leader to have a bad month and close the gap without ever seeming to hit top gear. That is what January was. City did not go on some blazing run that overwhelmed the league. They simply kept winning while Arsenal kept drawing, and the arithmetic did the rest. A two-point lead at the end of January with City as the team applying the pressure is a very different feeling than the cushion Arsenal carried into the new year.

What I think this means is that the title race is now a genuine two-team question in a way it had stopped being, and the pressure has shifted onto the leaders. Arsenal had spent months as the team whose lead I trusted. January turned them back into the team that has to prove it can hold on, which is the role they have struggled in before. How they respond to a shrinking lead is the exact thing they could not answer in past seasons, and now they have to answer it in real time with City breathing on them.

## The transfer window, as usual, resolved less than the noise

January also brought the transfer window, and I will say what I say about the January window almost every year, which is that it almost never fixes what a title race needs fixing. A team whose problem is a three-week scoring drought is not going to buy its way out of that in the middle of the season, because the players who could actually change a title team are not available in January and the ones who are available take months to settle. The window is mostly for patching squads that are thin and for clubs at the bottom trying to buy survival, and the churn around it generates far more noise than it does actual change at the top. I would be surprised if anything that happened in the window mattered more to the title than whether Arsenal's forwards rediscover their finishing in February.

The pattern I would pull out of January is that the season finally got hard for the team leading it. The vulnerability I had been describing since October arrived on schedule, the lead shrank to almost nothing, and Manchester City moved into exactly the position they like to be in. From here it is a run-in, and run-ins are decided by which of the two teams at the top blinks. Arsenal have the better defense and the harder question to answer about their nerve. City have the pedigree and the momentum. I still lean Arsenal, because the floor is so high that even their bad month only cost them a few points, but I am a lot less sure than I was at Christmas, and that is what makes February onward worth watching.
