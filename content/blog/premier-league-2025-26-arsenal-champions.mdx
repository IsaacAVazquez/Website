# Arsenal Are Champions: The End of a 22-Year Wait, and Why This Title Was Built the Right Way

Arsenal are Premier League champions, their first title since 2004, and after following this team through the whole season and writing down what I thought at every stage, I want to be honest that the thing I feel most is a kind of vindication of a story I had been telling since the autumn. Arsenal did not win this in a way that surprised me. They won it in almost exactly the way I said they would if they ever managed to, which is on the strength of a defense that did not break, and getting to watch a season-long argument actually pay off is a rare and satisfying thing.

The bare facts are these. Arsenal finished on 85 points, seven clear of Manchester City in second, and lifted the trophy on the final day after a 2-1 win away at Crystal Palace. Twenty-two years is a long time to wait, long enough that a generation of supporters has grown up knowing the club only as the team that comes close, and the number that will get repeated most is that gap back to 2004, the last time the title went to this part of North London. For a club that has spent the better part of a decade finishing second or third and being told it did not have the nerve for the final push, ending the drought is bigger than any single season's achievement. It changes what the club is allowed to believe about itself.

## They won it the way I trusted all along

What I keep coming back to is how this title was constructed, because it matters to me that it was not a fluke of hot finishing or a lucky run of results. Arsenal led the league for months on the back of not conceding, and every time I wrote about them I said the same thing, that a defensive lead is the kind I believe because it does not depend on anyone staying hot. The one danger I flagged over and over was a scoring drought letting the chasing pack back in, and in January that danger arrived exactly on cue, the goals dried up, and the lead that had looked commanding shrank to almost nothing with City applying the pressure they always apply.

The reason Arsenal are champions and not runners-up again is what they did after that. When City nudged ahead on goals late in the run-in and the season started to look like every near-miss that came before it, Arsenal answered with a run of wins without conceding, the two things they needed at once, and took the title back and then pulled clear. That is the sequence that had been missing from all those second-place seasons. The talent was never the question. The question was whether this team could take a punch in the closing weeks and respond instead of folding, and this year, for the first time, it responded. A seven-point final margin makes it look comfortable in hindsight, but it was not comfortable in April, and the gap is a measure of how strongly they finished rather than how easy it was.

## What changed, as far as I can tell

If I try to name what was different about this Arsenal team compared to the ones that fell short, I do not think it was a single signing or a tactical revolution. It was that the floor got higher and the nerve got steadier. David Raya's form in goal turned draws into wins all season, the back line in front of him held its shape through the winter congestion that usually cracks title challengers, and when the decisive weeks came the team played like it had done this before even though it had not. Mikel Arteta has spent years being questioned for exactly the thing this team finally delivered, the ability to close, and I think the fair verdict is that the patience the club showed in sticking with a project through the near-misses is a large part of why the project eventually won.

There is a version of this where I spend a few hundred words on what it means for City, or for the balance of power in the league, and I will get to some of that when I write the season in review. But the day a 22-year wait ends is not the day for that. It is the day to say plainly that Arsenal were the best team over 38 games, that they were the best team in the specific way that is hardest to sustain and most deserving when it holds, and that they got over a line they had been stuck behind for a very long time.

The pattern I would pull out of the whole season is that the title went to the team with the highest floor and the steadiest nerve, not the team with the most spectacular attack or the most expensive squad, and I find that a genuinely satisfying way for a league to be decided. Arsenal did not talk their way to this title or buy their way to it in a January panic. They built a defense that did not break and then, in the one stretch where their old failing threatened to return, they refused to fail. That is a champion, and after 22 years it is a deserved one.
