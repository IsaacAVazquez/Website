## Why This Project Exists

Most March Madness content falls into one of two buckets: generic bracket advice that mostly repeats seed-line history, or highly technical analytics that don't translate into an actual picking workflow. I wanted something in between, a product that helps you build a bracket by showing the strongest calls, but also explains the structure underneath those calls.

The key premise is simple. Seed lines are useful, but they are not the answer, because the committee misses teams every year, injuries shift matchups, and travel matters, and in a one-and-done format those small structural edges can matter more than broad brand perception.

## Best Upset Picks In This Bracket

I think UCF over UCLA is the best first-round upset on the board. UCLA takes a full three-time-zone jump from Pacific to Eastern time, which carries a 9% penalty inside this model. The betting spread is already tight enough that the travel hit flips the game cleanly. This is exactly the kind of matchup where the public still sees the brand name and misses the structural disadvantage.

St. John's over Kansas is the upset I trust most on seed error. St. John's grades stronger than a normal 5-seed, while Kansas is one of the most overseeded teams in the field. If you treat the 4-vs-5 line as a neutral matchup rather than proof of Kansas superiority, the upset case gets much stronger.

The later-round swing I keep coming back to is Houston over Florida. Houston gets a home regional and carries essentially no accumulated travel burden into the matchup, while Florida loses efficiency on the trip west. When the analytics gap is already narrow, that structural edge matters more than brand-name momentum.

## Final Four And Champion

The Final Four is Duke, Michigan, Arizona, and Houston. The champion is Duke.

Duke combines the best blended ranking set across the major public analytics systems, a 0% total travel penalty through its path, and the strongest defensive profile in the field. Arizona reaches the Final Four on a clean Pacific path, then gets hit with a major Pacific-to-Eastern travel penalty in Indianapolis. The short version is that Duke has the best profile with the least friction.

## How The Model Works

This bracket is built from four inputs layered together. The rankings blend KenPom with BPI, Evan Miya, KPI, NET, SOR, T-Rank, and WAB, which keeps the bracket from overreacting to one system. On top of that sits seed-error analysis. The committee misses every year, and Vanderbilt and St. John's look meaningfully better than their seed lines, while teams like Kansas and Alabama get more credit than the underlying numbers support. Injury context matters most in coin-flip games, where a missing starter or thin rotation can shift the actual signal. And then there is the time-zone layer, which is where this model diverges from most bracket tools.

The time-zone model applies penalties for east-west travel and final-slot games. That is why UCF over UCLA and Gonzaga over Purdue show up as real edges instead of novelty picks. ## Why Time Zones Matter More Than People Admit

A lot of bracket analysis is really ranking analysis in disguise. This project gets more interesting where the rankings are close and the travel burden is not. That is where the time-zone layer earns its place. It does not replace the consensus metrics, but it changes how narrow matchups should be interpreted. In the 2026 field, that shift is large enough to produce cleaner upset logic than seed lines alone would suggest.

## Region Notes

Duke has the cleanest path in the field and St. John's is the team most likely to ruin a conventional bracket in the East. In the West, Arizona owns the best regional path but Gonzaga over Purdue is the sharpest late-round call because Purdue pays the travel bill in a bad slot. Houston gets the strongest structural edge in the entire bracket because of geography, and Vanderbilt is the underseeded team worth trusting in the South. Michigan has the best defensive route through the Midwest, and Alabama's defense is too weak to survive deep into the bracket.
