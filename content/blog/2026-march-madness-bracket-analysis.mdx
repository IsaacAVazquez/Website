## Best Upset Picks In This 2026 March Madness Bracket

### 1. UCF over UCLA

This is the best first-round upset on the board because UCLA takes a full three-time-zone jump from Pacific to Eastern time. That is a 9% penalty inside this model, and the betting spread is already tight enough for the travel hit to flip the game cleanly.

Why it matters:

- UCLA is not losing because of a random hot take; it is losing because the travel math changes the baseline.
- The line is short enough that even a modest penalty matters.
- This is exactly the kind of game where the public still sees the brand name and misses the structural disadvantage.

### 2. St. John's over Kansas

This is the best seed-error upset in the bracket. St. John's grades stronger than a normal 5-seed, while Kansas is one of the most overseeded teams in the field. If you treat the 4-vs-5 line as a neutral matchup instead of as proof of Kansas superiority, the upset case gets much stronger.

### 3. Houston over Florida

This is the biggest later-round swing in the bracket. Houston gets a home regional and carries essentially no accumulated travel burden into the matchup, while Florida loses efficiency on the trip west. When the analytics gap is already narrow, that structural edge matters more than brand-name momentum.

## How This Bracket Model Works

This bracket is built from four inputs, not one:

### KenPom and consensus analytics

The rankings blend KenPom with BPI, Evan Miya, KPI, NET, SOR, T-Rank, and WAB. That keeps the bracket from overreacting to one system.

### S-curve seed errors

The committee misses every year. Vanderbilt and St. John's look better than their seed lines, while teams like Kansas and Alabama get more credit from the bracket than the underlying numbers support.

### Injury context

A missing starter or thin rotation matters more in coin-flip games than people want to admit. Injury adjustments help separate real upset signals from generic chaos.

### Time-zone travel penalties

This is the differentiator. The model applies penalties for east-west travel and final-slot games, which changes the effective output of teams crossing the country. That is why UCF over UCLA and Gonzaga over Purdue show up as real edges instead of novelty picks.
