# Premier League 2025-26 in February: The Run-In Is Not One Race, It's Four

The title race takes up most of the oxygen this time of year, and I have given it plenty of my own attention over the last two check-ins, but by the end of February the thing I actually wanted to write down is that the season had quietly split into four separate races, and three of them were more open than the one at the top. When I open [the dashboard](/premier-league) now, I am not really looking at first place, I am looking at the clusters, the group of clubs fighting for the Champions League places, the group below them chasing the European spots, and the group at the bottom trying not to go down. Each of those is its own run-in with its own math, and February was the month they all took their real shape.

## The scramble for the Champions League places

The most compelling of the four, to me, is the fight for the top five, because there are more good teams than there are spots and the margins are thin. Below Arsenal and Manchester City, who are running their own two-team race for the title, there is a cluster of clubs separated by only a few points, and the ordering of that cluster is going to swing on a handful of results over the next couple of months. Aston Villa have been the steadiest of them, doing the unglamorous thing of just accumulating points without much drama, which is exactly how a club that is not quite at the level of the top two sneaks into the Champions League anyway. Manchester United, who I want to say more about next month, have been climbing in a way that surprised me given where they started the season. And Liverpool, the defending champions, have spent the winter trying to stabilize a title defense that came apart, which has turned their season from a defense of the crown into a fight to hold on to a top-five place, a fall I still find a little startling to type.

What makes this race worth watching is that the gap between finishing in the Champions League and finishing just outside it is enormous, both financially and for a club's sense of its own trajectory, and it is going to come down to which of these teams handles the pressure of the run-in best. That is a different skill from being good over 38 games, and it does not always favor the team with the best players.

## The European places and the promoted-club surprise

A rung below that, there is a race for the Europa League and Conference League places, and this is where one of the stories I have been following all season pays off. Sunderland, who I have written about more than any other club this year because their promotion season kept refusing to follow the script, are not fighting relegation the way promoted play-off teams are supposed to. They are up in the part of the table where European qualification is a real possibility, which for a club that came up through the play-offs is close to unthinkable. I keep waiting for the fall that never comes, and at this point I have mostly stopped waiting.

## The relegation battle finds its shape

At the bottom, February is where the relegation picture stops being a loose worry and becomes a specific fight among specific clubs. The winter congestion that I said in December would start to strain the shallowest squads did exactly that, and the bottom of the table by the end of February had a settled, worrying look for a few clubs, including one of the promoted three whose early competitiveness could not survive the grind, and at least one established club having the kind of season that gets managers changed and supporters angry. I am not going to name the doomed this early, because relegation battles have a way of turning on one good run in March, but the shape of the fight was clear, and the clubs in it knew who they were.

The pattern I would pull out of February is that a season is never really one story, it is four or five overlapping ones, and the run-in is the stretch where all of them get urgent at the same time. The title race narrowed to a two-team staring contest, the Champions League places became a genuine scramble, the European race produced a promoted club I did not think would be anywhere near it, and the relegation fight hardened into a small group of clubs with real reason to be afraid. Every one of those is going to be decided in the next two and a half months, and most of them are closer than the one everybody is watching.
