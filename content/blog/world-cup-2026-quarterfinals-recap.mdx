# World Cup 2026 Quarterfinals: The Seeds Struck Back and Morocco's Run Finally Ended

For three weeks this tournament read like an upset factory. The group stage bent the seeds, the [Round of 32](/writing/world-cup-2026-round-of-32-recap) let the favorites through on a technicality, and then the [Round of 16](/writing/world-cup-2026-round-of-16-recap) went and put Germany, the Netherlands, and Brazil out, three of the ten teams I thought could win the whole thing gone before the last eight was even set. So I want to be honest about what the quarterfinals actually did, because it is the opposite of what the previous ten days trained everyone to expect. The last eight quietly reassembled the pre-tournament hierarchy. All four of my [pre-tournament top four](/writing/world-cup-2026-top-ten-contenders), Spain, France, England, and Argentina, are in the semifinals, and the two dark-horse runs that made this bracket fun both ended in the same round.

What I think this means is that 48 teams widened the field without changing who tends to be standing at the end. The extra places bought us more first-round drama and a longer path, but the path still funneled toward the teams that were supposed to be here. That is worth sitting with before the semifinals talk us back into chaos.

## France ended Morocco's run the way France ends things

The tie everyone circled was [France](/world-cup-2026?team=france) against [Morocco](/world-cup-2026?team=morocco), partly because it echoed their 2022 semifinal and partly because Morocco had spent a month proving the echo did not have to repeat. It repeated anyway. France won it two-nil, and the scoreline undersells how in control they were. Morocco had knocked out the Netherlands and then dismantled host Canada, and I kept waiting for the giant-killing to run out of road, and this was the road. France did the thing the best teams do against a dangerous underdog, which is win the game without ever letting it become the kind of game the underdog needs it to be.

I had Morocco ninth, and a team I ranked ninth reaching the quarterfinals by beating a top-eight side and a host on the way is a genuinely good tournament, not a disappointment. But the gap between a run and a title is exactly the gap Morocco met on Friday. France have the players to raise the ceiling when the game demands it, and Morocco, for all their organization, did not quite have the second gear to answer.

## England outlasted Norway, and Norway made them earn every minute of it

The other top-half tie was [England](/world-cup-2026?team=england) against [Norway](/world-cup-2026?team=norway), and after what Norway did to Brazil a round earlier I said I would not walk into this one assuming England stroll through. They did not stroll. England won two-one, and Norway, who I had flagged for winning ugly and refusing to hand games back, once again made a favorite sweat out the full ninety. Erling Haaland's tournament ends in the quarterfinals, which is roughly where the math said a Norway team built around one extraordinary forward would top out, and I mean that as a compliment to how far the one-forward model carried them.

England advancing without convincing anyone is the most England sentence I can write, and I still think it counts. Scoring enough to beat a side that just put Brazil out, on a night where the game was closer than the seeding suggested, is a harder thing to do than the neutral gives England credit for. They are in the semifinals, and the question of whether they can win the game that actually matters is still open, which is also the most England sentence I can write.

## Spain and Argentina did what the top two seeds on the bottom half do

The bottom half went closer to script. [Spain](/world-cup-2026?team=spain), the team I ranked first and have not talked myself out of, beat [Belgium](/world-cup-2026?team=belgium) two-one in the one bottom-half tie that carried real jeopardy, and they did it the way they have done everything this tournament, by keeping the ball long enough that the opponent's best spell never arrives. Belgium's golden generation gets its last shot and comes up a round short of the semifinals, which will read as a disappointment and probably is one, though losing a one-goal game to the best team in the field is not the same as being exposed.

[Argentina](/world-cup-2026?team=argentina) were the more comfortable of the two, beating [Switzerland](/world-cup-2026?team=switzerland) three-one, and the story there is the one I flagged before the tournament, which is that this is the last act of Lionel Messi and Argentina keep finding another gear when it is. Switzerland were organized and awkward and exactly the kind of side that ends careers a round early, and Argentina simply had too much at the top of the pitch. They are two wins from sending Messi out on top, and I stopped betting against that particular story a long time ago.

## What the semifinals set up

So the semifinals are Spain against France and England against Argentina, which is the top four seeds in the last four, and I did not expect to be typing that after the Round of 16 tried to burn the bracket down. Spain against France is the tie of the tournament on paper, the two most complete sides in the field, and I lean Spain narrowly because their control travels and France's path has asked less of them. England against Argentina is Messi against the one big nation that keeps almost getting there, and it is the semifinal I would least confidently call.

The pattern I would pull out of the quarterfinals is that this tournament has been two different competitions stacked on top of each other. The first three weeks belonged to Morocco and Norway and the teams who made 48 feel like a genuine reshuffle, and the last eight belonged to the four teams that were always most likely to be here. Expanding the field changed how we got to the semifinals. It did not, in the end, change who arrived.
