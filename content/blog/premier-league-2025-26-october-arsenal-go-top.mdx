# Premier League 2025-26 in October: Arsenal Take Over at the Top

Two months ago I said the two teams I was watching most closely were the loud one and the quiet one, Liverpool making the early running and Arsenal sitting just behind without any drama. Through October the quiet one moved to the front, and the way they did it is the reason I think this is the month the title race actually took its shape rather than just its noise.

Arsenal went top not by outscoring everyone in a run of wild games but by more or less refusing to concede. When I open [the dashboard](/premier-league) and pull up their recent form, the thing that jumps out is not the goals they were scoring, it is the goals they were not letting in. A team that goes on a run of clean sheets is telling you something different from a team that goes on a run of high-scoring wins. The high-scoring run says a team is in hot form and you wonder how long the finishing holds up. The clean-sheet run says a team has an organizing principle that does not depend on anyone being hot, and that is the kind of thing that survives a bad afternoon in front of goal.

## Why leading through defense is the lead I believe

I have come around over the years to trusting defensive records more than attacking ones when I am trying to figure out whether a team at the top is going to stay there. Attacking form is streaky by nature, because it leans on finishing and finishing regresses. A back line and a goalkeeper that are not giving up chances tend to keep not giving up chances, because that is a function of structure and habits rather than luck. So when Arsenal took over top spot on the back of not conceding, I read it as a more durable kind of lead than the one Liverpool had built earlier on the strength of simply winning a lot of games quickly.

That is not a knock on Liverpool, who were still very much in it and are still the defending champions with all the habits that come with that. It is a statement about which kind of front-running I would bet on holding up. If you had asked me in August whether Arsenal could finally convert their perennial closeness into an actual lead, I would have said the talent was never the question, the question was whether they had an identity that did not wobble. October was the first real evidence that they might.

## The chasing pack sorted itself out too

October also did some sorting behind Arsenal. Manchester City, who I have deliberately not said much about yet, were doing the thing City tend to do, which is quietly stay within range without dominating the early narrative, and I would never write them off from within touching distance in the autumn because they have made a habit of being exactly there and then finishing over the top. Liverpool were still in the mix. And the promoted clubs, which I spent all of last month on, kept holding their positions well enough that I did not have to walk back anything I said about them, which was a small relief.

What I keep coming back to about this month is how little drama there was to Arsenal getting to the top. There was no signature October where they blew someone away and announced themselves. They just kept not losing and kept not conceding until one day they were in front, and the table had rearranged itself around a team that had barely raised its voice. That is usually how the most solid leads get built, and it is almost always less fun to watch than a team scoring five.

The pattern I would pull out of October is that the title race now has a front-runner whose lead I actually trust, which is a different situation from a front-runner who is simply hot. Arsenal are top because of the part of the game that is least likely to desert them. The obvious risk, and I will keep an eye on it, is that a team built on not conceding can go through spells where it also cannot score, and those spells turn wins into draws and let the chasing pack back in. That is a January problem, though, or at least I hoped it was. In October the thing to notice was simply that the quiet team had moved to the front, and had done it in the way that tends to last.
