## How the ranking was built (methodology)

The order below is a synthesis, not a copy of any single board. It weighs four spines that largely agree at the top:

1. **Betting markets** — outright title odds across DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, Bet365, plus no-vig prediction markets (Kalshi, Polymarket), as of June 2–8, 2026.
2. **FIFA World Ranking** — April 1, 2026 edition (next update June 11).
3. **Opta supercomputer** — ~10,000-simulation model published mid-May 2026 (title probabilities).
4. **Form + squad + draw** — final qualifying and June 2026 warm-ups, the announced 26-man squads, and how hard each team's group and projected knockout path is.

Where the spines disagree (Spain vs France at #1, Brazil vs Portugal at #5/6, the #9/#10 bubble), I made an editorial call and documented the reasoning so it is defensible in print.

## Per-country dossiers

### 1. Spain 🇪🇸 — Group H (Uruguay, Cape Verde, Saudi Arabia)

- **History.** One World Cup (2010), but reigning European champions (Euro 2024) and the most decorated era marker in the modern game alongside the 2008-2012 run. Recent World Cups have stung: a group-stage exit as holders in 2014, round-of-16 losses in 2018 (Russia) and 2022 (on penalties to Morocco). The pedigree question is knockout nerve, not talent.
- **Squad / manager.** Luis de la Fuente named a 26 with **no Real Madrid players for the first time ever**. The spine is Barcelona-and-Arsenal heavy: Lamine Yamal, Pedri, Gavi, Pau Cubarsí, plus Rodri (Manchester City), Martín Zubimendi and Mikel Merino (Arsenal), Nico Williams, Dani Olmo, Mikel Oyarzabal. GKs Unai Simón, David Raya, Joan García. Yamal and Nico Williams carried late-season muscle injuries but were included and are on track for the opener.
- **Likelihood / path.** Highest qualification probability in the field (~98.5% per Opta) but a genuinely tough group because Uruguay is a fellow contender as the #2 seed. Spain sits on the softer left half of the bracket (Dallas semifinal) and is the only team Opta rates better than even money to reach the quarters (52.1%). Opta title leader at 16.1%.
- **Betting.** Co-favorite at +450 to +475, the widest inter-book spread of any top team (FanDuel +430 vs BetMGM +500, so shop BetMGM). "Reach semifinal" +115 is near even money, the cleanest way the market expresses Spain as the class of the field. Heavy group favorite at roughly -310.
- **Matchups.** Spain vs Uruguay (Group H) is the marquee group game and likely decides top spot. As group winner, the projected round-of-32 is a third-place qualifier; the half could yield a France or Germany collision deep.
- **Verdict.** The #1. Best squad, friendliest path, only real worry is the recurring knockout fragility and Yamal's fitness.

### 2. France 🇫🇷 — Group I (Senegal, Norway, Iraq) — GROUP OF DEATH

- **History.** Two titles (1998, 2018) and the 2022 runners-up after losing a final-for-the-ages to Argentina on penalties. The deepest talent pool in world football for a decade.
- **Squad / manager.** Didier Deschamps named his squad May 14 in what he has confirmed is his **final tournament** in charge. Kylian Mbappé captains and chases the national scoring record; reigning Ballon d'Or winner Ousmane Dembélé and Michael Olise headline a ludicrous attacking depth (Barcola, Cherki, Doué, Thuram, Mateta). Eduardo Camavinga was a notable omission after a poor club season.
- **Likelihood / path.** FIFA #1 and Opta #2 (13.0%), but the draw is brutal. Group I has the highest average Opta power rating in the tournament and three top-25 sides (France, Senegal, Norway). France should still advance, but second place or worse is a live risk, which is exactly why the path is the hardest of any top seed.
- **Betting.** Co-favorite at +475 to +500; best price +500 at DraftKings/FanDuel. France has led the no-vig prediction markets (Kalshi, Polymarket) since late April. "Reach final" +240.
- **Matchups.** France vs Senegal and France vs Norway (Haaland vs Mbappé) are two of the best group games of the entire tournament. A potential France vs Netherlands or France vs Germany looms on the left side.
- **Verdict.** #2 only because of the group. On a neutral draw this is the #1. The talent is championship-grade; the route is a minefield from match one.

### 3. England 🏴 — Group L (Croatia, Ghana, Panama)

- **History.** One title (1966) and nothing since outside it: a 2018 semifinal, a 2022 quarterfinal loss to France, and back-to-back European Championship final defeats (2021, 2024). The story is always talent versus delivery.
- **Squad / manager.** Thomas Tuchel named a ruthless 26 at Wembley, cutting **Cole Palmer, Phil Foden, Trent Alexander-Arnold and Harry Maguire**. Harry Kane captains a third World Cup; Jude Bellingham, Bukayo Saka and Declan Rice anchor the core, with Eberechi Eze, Kobbie Mainoo and a recalled Ivan Toney for depth.
- **Likelihood / path.** The only European side to win all eight qualifiers (8-0-0). A clean third on every board (Opta 11.2%). Manageable group, though Croatia is a dangerous #2 and topping the group matters to avoid a nastier flip in the bracket. Projected to face a heavyweight quarterfinal (Brazil is the model favorite there).
- **Betting.** Clear third at +650 to +700; best price +700 DraftKings. The line wobbled on the squad announcement (a FanDuel snapshot showed +600). "Reach semifinal" +170.
- **Matchups.** England vs Croatia (Group L) is a 2018-semifinal rematch with real edge. A projected quarterfinal vs Brazil would be the headline of the round.
- **Verdict.** #3. The data backs it and the qualifying was flawless; the discount reflects a decade of falling just short.

### 4. Argentina 🇦🇷 — Group J (Algeria, Austria, Jordan)

- **History.** Three titles (1978, 1986, 2022) and the defending champions after beating France on penalties in Qatar. Back-to-back Copa América winners (2021, 2024).
- **Squad / manager.** Lionel Scaloni kept the spine of the Qatar winners and confirmed **Lionel Messi for his sixth and final World Cup**, adding fresh legs in Nico Paz, Valentín Barco and Giuliano Simeone. Ángel Di María and five other 2022 champions were left out. The cloud is Messi's age (38, turning 39 during the tournament) and a minor injury from late May that kept him out of the Honduras warm-up.
- **Likelihood / path.** Softest group of any top seed (~96.7% to advance) and FIFA #3, Opta #4 (10.4%). Sits in the opposite half from Spain, so the two can only meet in the final. The round-of-32 as group winner projects to a tricky opponent (likely Uruguay out of Group H).
- **Betting.** +800 to +900, tied with Brazil; best price +900, and the market has not settled which of the two is the "fifth" contender. "Reach semifinal" +210.
- **Matchups.** The dream is a Messi vs Ronaldo quarterfinal against Portugal, a storyline the bracket genuinely allows. Argentina vs Uruguay in the round of 32 would be a Río de la Plata classic.
- **Verdict.** #4. The ceiling is champion again and the Messi farewell is the best story in the sport; the floor is capped by an aging core and the fitness of a 38-year-old.

### 5. Brazil 🇧🇷 — Group C (Morocco, Haiti, Scotland)

- **History.** Five titles, the most ever, but the last was 2002. A 24-year drought that includes the 7-1 home humiliation in 2014 and quarterfinal exits in 2018 and 2022 (on penalties to Croatia).
- **Squad / manager.** Carlo Ancelotti, the first foreign coach to lead Brazil to a World Cup, named his 26 on May 18 in Rio. Vinícius Júnior is the focal point with Raphinha, Marquinhos as captain, and **Neymar recalled at 34**. The shock of the announcement was **Rodrygo left out entirely**, alongside João Pedro, Militão and Andrey Santos.
- **Likelihood / path.** FIFA #6, Opta #6 (6.6%). The group is trickier than a usual Brazil draw because Morocco is a contender in its own right, but Brazil is favored to top it. Right side of the bracket, with a projected quarterfinal against England.
- **Betting.** +800 to +900, level with Argentina; best price +900. "Reach final" +430.
- **Matchups.** Brazil vs Morocco (Group C) is a rematch of the friendly Morocco won in 2023 and a measuring stick. A projected England quarterfinal is the marquee.
- **Verdict.** #5. Higher ceiling and squad depth than Portugal, which is why I edge them ahead, but the Rodrygo call and a Neymar-at-34 gamble add real volatility.

### 6. Portugal 🇵🇹 — Group K (Colombia, DR Congo, Uzbekistan)

- **History.** No World Cup title (best finishes third in 1966, fourth in 2006), but Euro 2016 champions and Nations League winners. Perennially loaded, perennially short of a deep World Cup run.
- **Squad / manager.** Roberto Martínez named a symbolic 27+1 on May 19, the "+1" a tribute to the late Diogo Jota. **Cristiano Ronaldo captains a record sixth World Cup at 41**, chasing the one trophy that has eluded him. The real strength is the midfield: Bruno Fernandes (FWA Player of the Year), Vitinha, João Neves and Bernardo Silva, with Rafael Leão, Pedro Neto and João Félix up top.
- **Likelihood / path.** FIFA #5 and Opta #5 (7.0%), rated this high despite the second-toughest group in the tournament, the only one with two top-10 sides (Portugal and Colombia). That the model still favors them speaks to the squad's strength.
- **Betting.** +850 to +1000, the single biggest top-tier price discrepancy; take DraftKings +1000 over ESPN's +850, since Portugal was actively shortening. "Reach final" +460.
- **Matchups.** Portugal vs Colombia (Group K) likely decides the group. The bracket dangles a Ronaldo vs Messi quarterfinal against Argentina.
- **Verdict.** #6, essentially a coin flip with Brazil. The midfield may be the best in the field, but the team is older in key spots and the Ronaldo-centric questions never quite go away.

### 7. Germany 🇩🇪 — Group E (Curaçao, Ivory Coast, Ecuador)

- **History.** Four titles, last in 2014, then a stunning collapse: group-stage exits in both 2018 and 2022, and a home Euro 2024 quarterfinal loss to Spain. A proud program rebuilding its identity.
- **Squad / manager.** Julian Nagelsmann named his 26 on May 21. Joshua Kimmich captains; Jamal Musiala (recovered from a broken leg) and Florian Wirtz form an elite young creative axis, with Kai Havertz as a false nine. The headline was the **surprise recall of 40-year-old Manuel Neuer** after Ter Stegen's injury.
- **Likelihood / path.** FIFA #10 but priced higher by odds and Opta (5.1%) on talent. The draw is the softest of any contender by a distance: Germany's power rating dwarfs the group, so a clean run to the quarterfinals is on the table.
- **Betting.** +1400, the bridge between the elite and the mid-pack. The value case is the easy path rather than the headline price.
- **Matchups.** No marquee group game, which is the point. The first real test projects to a quarterfinal, potentially against France or Spain on the left side.
- **Verdict.** #7. Two straight group-stage exits demand caution, but the combination of Musiala-Wirtz and the kindest draw in the bracket is a live deep-run formula.

### 8. Netherlands 🇳🇱 — Group F (Japan, Sweden, Tunisia)

- **History.** Three World Cup finals (1974, 1978, 2010), never a title, the great nearly-men. A 2022 quarterfinal exit to Argentina on penalties and a third place in 2014.
- **Squad / manager.** Ronald Koeman named a Premier-League-heavy 26 on May 27 (15 of 26 based in England). Virgil van Dijk captains one of the deepest defenses in the tournament, with Frenkie de Jong and Cody Gakpo carrying the attack. The blow was **Xavi Simons rupturing his ACL in April**, removing the team's most creative player; Frimpong is also out.
- **Likelihood / path.** FIFA #7, Opta #8 (3.6%). Favored in the group (~48% to win it, ~88% to advance) but Japan and Sweden are credible. A medium draw on the left side, with a France collision possible later.
- **Betting.** +2000 to +2200; the first real long shot tier. Shop ESPN +2000 over FanDuel +2200.
- **Matchups.** Netherlands vs Japan (Group F) is a stylistic test against one of the tournament's best non-European sides. A projected round-of-32 against Morocco would be a rematch of the 2022 group meeting.
- **Verdict.** #8. The back line keeps them in the top ten; the Simons injury is why they sit clearly behind the front seven.

### 9. Morocco 🇲🇦 — Group C (Brazil, Haiti, Scotland)

- **History.** The story of 2022: the first African and Arab nation to reach a World Cup semifinal, beating Spain and Portugal on the way to fourth place. Ran to the AFCON 2025 final on home soil. Genuine, recent, knockout pedigree, which separates them from every other outsider.
- **Squad / manager.** A March earthquake: **Walid Regragui, the architect of 2022, resigned on March 5, 2026**, three months out. Mohamed Ouahbi stepped up from the U20 side he led to the 2025 U20 World Cup title (beating Argentina 2-0 in the final) and named the 26 on May 26. Achraf Hakimi captains; Brahim Díaz is the creative engine after a standout AFCON; Yassine Bounou and Sofyan Amrabat headline nine returnees from the 2022 run. En-Nesyri and Boufal missed out.
- **Likelihood / path.** FIFA #8, the top-ranked African side, but Opta only #11-12 (1.93%) given the coaching upheaval and a group with Brazil. Likely to advance, probably second, into a medium-hard round of 32.
- **Betting.** +5000; longer than Belgium and Colombia despite the better FIFA rank and pedigree, which is the value argument for backers who trust the 2022 template survives the coaching change.
- **Matchups.** Morocco vs Brazil (Group C) is the showcase, a rematch of the 2023 friendly Morocco won 2-1. A projected Netherlands round-of-32 would echo their Qatar group.
- **Verdict.** #9. The pedigree and the spine (Hakimi, Bounou, Brahim, Amrabat) earn the spot; the new manager is the swing factor between a repeat run and an early flameout.

### 10. Belgium 🇧🇪 — Group G (Egypt, Iran, New Zealand)

- **History.** No title, but a third place in 2018 was the golden generation's peak before a flat group-stage exit in 2022. A program managing the long goodbye of a brilliant cohort.
- **Squad / manager.** French coach Rudi Garcia named the 26 on May 15, keeping faith with the veterans. Thibaut Courtois, Kevin De Bruyne, Jérémy Doku and Leandro Trossard lead, with Romelu Lukaku included despite an injury-hit season and his strong World Cup scoring record.
- **Likelihood / path.** FIFA #9, Opta #10 (2.35%). The softest realistic floor of the bubble: a comfortable Group G (favorites at ~-110 to win it, ~89% to advance) all but guarantees knockout football, where a one-off with Courtois in goal and De Bruyne pulling strings is dangerous.
- **Betting.** +3500 to +4000, the shortest of the bubble teams, which is why they make the ten over the higher-ceiling, lower-floor outsiders.
- **Matchups.** No marquee group game, but a projected round-of-16 against a heavyweight (Spain has been floated) would be the test of whether the golden generation has one last run in it.
- **Verdict.** #10. Not the highest ceiling, but the safest passage to the knockouts plus residual quality plus the "last dance" narrative make them the most bankable of the outsiders.

## Dark horses and just-missed

- **Norway** (Group I) — the marquee dark horse and Opta's favorite outsider (~3.3-3.5%, ahead of Belgium). First World Cup since 1998, ending a 28-year exile, with Erling Haaland in career form (55 international goals, 16 in qualifying) and Martin Ødegaard captaining. Why they missed the ten: zero tournament pedigree for this group and the Group of Death draw, where finishing third and going home is a live outcome. Highest ceiling, lowest floor of any near-contender.
- **Colombia** (Group K) — FIFA #13, Opta 2.1%, +4000. James Rodríguez (2014 Golden Boot) captains and Luis Díaz leads the line. Drew Portugal's tough group, which caps the path, but a very live round-of-32 team.
- **Uruguay** (Group H) — Bielsa's side with Valverde, Araújo, Bentancur and Darwin Núñez; Muslera came out of retirement for a fifth World Cup, Luis Suárez did not make it. Drew straight into Spain's group, the hardest possible path for a #2 seed.
- **Croatia** (Group L) — 2018 finalists and 2022 third place, the eternal tournament overachievers, but aging and long in the market (+7000-8000). The pedigree pick.
- **Host note: USA** (~+6000, Group D) — drew the hardest-to-call group (Turkey, Australia, Paraguay), lost the final warm-up 1-2 to Germany under Mauricio Pochettino, and slipped to FIFA #16. A sentiment play, not a contender by the numbers. Mexico (+6500) and Canada (+17500) got soft groups but rate as long shots.
- **Notable absentee: Italy** — FIFA #12 but failed to qualify, losing the March playoff to Bosnia & Herzegovina on penalties. A top-12 side watching from home.

**Format:** 48 teams, 12 groups of 4. Top two plus the eight best third-place teams reach a new round of 32. Group stage June 11-27; round of 32 June 28-July 3; semifinals at Dallas (July 14) and Atlanta (July 15); final July 19 at MetLife Stadium. From the quarterfinals on, every match is in the USA. FIFA seeded the bracket so Spain and Argentina can only meet in the final, and the top four seeds can only meet in the semis. Left half (Dallas SF): France, Germany, Spain, Netherlands. Right half (Atlanta SF): Brazil, England, Argentina, Portugal.

**Best draws:** Germany (E), Mexico (A), Canada (B), Argentina (J). **Worst draws:** France (I, Group of Death), Portugal (K, with Colombia), USA (D, coin-flip), Spain/Uruguay (H, two heavyweights).

## Freshness caveats (read before publishing)

- **Odds move hourly.** All title/group/reach-round prices are dated June 2-8, 2026. Re-pull before stating a number in a published piece, and frame betting content as analysis, not advice.
- **FIFA points.** The April ranking order is solid; exact point totals for several ranks were not cleanly sourced. The next ranking publishes June 11 and may reshuffle the order.
- **Knockout opponents are projections.** Round-of-32 pairings, especially which third-place teams slot where, are not fixed until the group stage ends June 27. Always label these as projected.
- **Squad detail.** All 26-man squads are announced and sourced, but late injury withdrawals are still possible up to kickoff (watch Yamal/Nico Williams for Spain and Messi's fitness for Argentina). One conflicting report on Brazil's forwards was resolved in favor of "Rodrygo omitted."
- **Opta simulation count.** Sources cite both 10,000 and 25,000 simulations; the probabilities are consistent across outlets either way.
